## Multi-Market Story Structure

### Overview

Multi-market stories feature **multiple prediction markets** that open and close at different points during the timeline, unlike single-market stories where one primary market resolves at the end.

### Key Characteristics

1. **Multiple Markets**: Several distinct prediction markets operate simultaneously or sequentially
2. **Shorter Durations**: Individual markets may have shorter durations than the overall story timeline
3. **Independent Resolution**: Markets can resolve independently based on different criteria
4. **Dynamic Opening**: New markets open as new information emerges or events occur
5. **Mixed Outcomes**: Some markets may resolve YES while others resolve NO in the same story outcome
6. **Follow-Up Markets**: New markets can open after resolution based on revelations

### When to Use Multi-Market Structure

Use multi-market structure when:
- **Multiple resolvable questions** emerge throughout the timeline
- **Different aspects** of the story can be bet on independently
- **Technical milestones** create separate betting opportunities
- **Individual elements** (like names, events, outcomes) can be resolved separately
- **Story complexity** benefits from parallel market activity

### Market Lifecycle Management

For each market, track:
- **Opening Time**: When the market opens (specific day/time in timeline)
- **Opening Trigger**: What event or information caused the market to open
- **Resolution Criteria**: Clear criteria for YES/NO resolution
- **Closing Time**: When the market resolves (may be before story ends)
- **Dependencies**: Whether resolution depends on other markets or story outcomes

### Example Structure

**Day 1:** Primary market opens (e.g., "Will X happen?")
**Day 2:** Secondary market opens (e.g., "Is Y true?")
**Day 3:** Multiple parallel markets open (e.g., individual name markets)
**Day 4:** Technical market opens (e.g., "Will Z decode?")
**Day 5:** Follow-up market opens (e.g., "Was W the cause?")
**Day 6:** Final market opens (e.g., "Will it happen THIS WEEK?")
**Day 7:** All markets resolve based on outcome

### Resolution Logic

Markets resolve based on:
- **Specific criteria** for each market (not just overall story outcome)
- **What actually happened** (not what was promised or expected)
- **Independent verification** (each market has its own resolution logic)
- **Partial resolutions** (some elements confirmed, others not)
- **Unexpected events** (leaks, revelations, technical breakthroughs)
- **Timing is unpredictable** — resolutions happen when evidence emerges, not on a schedule

**Final Resolutions (Day 7):**

This creates a **cascading resolution pattern** where each resolution creates new information that affects remaining markets.

## Multi-Market Story Guidelines

### When to Open Markets

Markets should open when:
- **New information emerges** that creates a resolvable question
- **Public speculation** reaches a threshold
- **A specific event** is scheduled (vote, release, etc.)
- **Technical milestones** are reached (decryption, authentication, etc.)

### Market Duration

- Markets can be **shorter than the overall story timeline**
- Some markets may resolve **before the story ends** (e.g., "Will Folder 0 decode this week?")
- Other markets resolve **at the final outcome** (e.g., name markets)
- Markets should have **clear resolution criteria**

### Independent Resolution

- Markets can resolve **independently** of each other
- Some markets may resolve YES while others resolve NO
- Resolution depends on **specific criteria** for each market
- Example: Vote market resolves YES, but name markets may resolve NO if names aren't in released files

### Follow-Up Markets

- New markets can open **after resolution** based on revelations
- Follow-up markets explore **consequences** of the outcome
- Examples: "Will there be prosecutions?" "Will international relations be damaged?"
